Physics Experiment

Physical Reasoning: Helicopter Cable Problem

Various models are posed a variation of a problem from the 2014 qualifying exam for the United States physics team, in which the examinees are asked which shape a uniform flexible cable will adopt if suspended from a helicopter with a constant horizontal speed, where are resistance is non-negligible.

The objective is to assess the capability of LLMs to solve difficult problems, particularly those which require an intuitive understanding of physical interactions over a standard formulaic approach.

Such problems require models to apply a logical chain of thought rather than recall and apply a formula.

Grading System

The responses of each model were graded based on their correctness; while many models failed the task, some models exhibited a greater understanding than others. Of the successful models, the highest grade was assigned to those that arrived at the correct answer with the most concise derivations.

Cable Shape Prediction vs. Model Scale (Total Parameters)

Analysis Categories:

  • Correct (Straight Line): The model correctly derived that the constant force vectors result in a straight line.
  • Close/Plausible: The model recognized both forces but failed the mathematical simplification.
  • Standard Error: The model defaulted to common textbook answers (Catenary, Parabola).
  • Hallucination: The model predicted mathematically unrelated shapes.
